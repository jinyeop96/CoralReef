Our Finding

This visualization was created to show the tendency of coral reefs to be influenced by other factors

Number of coral reefs VS Human density

We can see from the figure that the main points are distributed in the population density of 0-3500000, and the points that exceed the population density of 3500000 are very sparse. Although some points are quite numerous, we can believe that coral reefs are more suitable for growth in the population density range of 0-3500000 due to the influence of other factors.

Coral Reef Count by Ph Range

The chart show how coral reefs are affected at different Ph ranges (mean and median). In the ph range between 8 and 9, coral reefs are surviving well, coral reefs are very sensitive to water ph and if the ph value go below 8 or above 9, the number of coral reefs declines dramatically.

Coral Reef Count by Temperature Range

The chart show how coral reefs are affected at different Temperature ranges (mean and median). It can be seen that the living environment of coral reefs is relatively stable between 23 and 27 degrees, but the number of coral reefs starts to decline slowly when the temperature is lower than 23 degrees, while the number of coral reefs above 30 degrees drops sharply.

Coral Reef Count by Water Sal density

This pie chart shows the effects on coral reefs at different water and salt densities. We can see in the chart that the proportion of coral reefs is still high when the water and salt density is between 38.5-40, which proves that coral reefs are suitable for survival in the 38.5-40 range. As water and salt densities rise above 41, coral reef numbers continue to decline.
