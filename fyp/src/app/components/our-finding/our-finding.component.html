The impact of COVID-19 on the United States

This visualization was created to show the impact COVID-19 is having on states across the United States

Positive Case status by state (September 2021)

We can see the distribution of cases in the states of the United States by the color of this graph. For example, there are more than 1 million cases in Taxes, California, and Florida, while there are less than 200,000 cases in Montana.

Vaccination status by state (September 2021)

The map on the left shows the distribution of vaccination rates by state in the United States. Because of the imbalance of population, percentage is used to calculate. States like Maine and Vermont have vaccination rates as high as almost 70 percent. But in, areas such asWyoming and La Daho do not reach even half that rate.

Death toll by state

This bar chart shows the death toll by state in the United States. Notably, New York had a particularly high number of deaths (27,149), while Vermont had only 79. There are two main reasons for this, one is the epidemic prevention measures, the other is the population base.

Death VS Positive Cases

This Scatter Plot shows a linear increase in the number of deaths and positive cases. We can see that there is a very obvious outlier (NewYork). I think the main reason is that there are so many people in NewYork who are dying before they get tested for COVID-19.
